What do we know about strategies to help students with ASD succeed in postsecondary education?

  • Knowing how ASD affects you and being brave enough to explain what you need to be successful to others is very important. You can connect with the Disability Resource Office at your institution to ask for academic accommodations. Every college, technical college, or university has an office like this–but they may be called something else, like Accessibility Services, Student Success Center, Office for Disabilities, or even Office of Diversity and Equity. These academic accommodations can include things that helped you in high school to do better on assignments and tests. It may be extra time on a written exam, or a quiet room for testing. It may also be where you sit in the class, or the ability to use a silent fidget to keep yourself focused in class.
  • Organization is the key to success, and an area where many students with ASD struggle. There are usually courses or groups you can participate in at your institution that will give you strategies to support executive functioning challenges often seen in ASD, but you must choose to take part–they will not come to you. Be a good detective and locate the resources to help you succeed. Many institutions have free tutoring, writing centers, and labs to help students succeed.
